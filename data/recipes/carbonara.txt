Spaghetti Carbonara is an iconic Italian pasta dish that has gained popularity all around the world. With its rich and creamy sauce, savory pancetta, and perfectly cooked pasta, this recipe is a true delight for pasta lovers. In this article, we’ll share an authentic Spaghetti Carbonara recipe, along with tips and tricks to help you create a delicious and authentic Italian dish in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients:
For the Spaghetti Carbonara, you will need:

8 ounces (225g) spaghetti
4 ounces (115g) pancetta or bacon, diced
3 large egg yolks
1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
2 garlic cloves, minced
Freshly ground black pepper
Salt to taste
Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
Instructions:

Cook the Spaghetti:
Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water; this will season the pasta as it cooks. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente, which means it should be cooked but still slightly firm to the bite. Drain the spaghetti, reserving some of the cooking water.

Prepare the Sauce:
In a large skillet, cook the pancetta or bacon over medium heat until crispy and golden brown. Remove the pancetta from the skillet, leaving the rendered fat behind.

Create the Carbonara Mixture:
In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Whisk until well combined and set aside.

Combine and Toss:
Place the drained spaghetti into the skillet with the rendered pancetta fat. Toss the spaghetti in the fat until it is well coated. This step allows the pasta to absorb the delicious flavors of the pancetta.

Add the Carbonara Mixture:
Remove the skillet from the heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the spaghetti. Toss the spaghetti vigorously, ensuring that the heat from the pasta cooks the eggs and creates a creamy sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, add a small amount of the reserved pasta cooking water to loosen it up.

Serve and Garnish:
Divide the Spaghetti Carbonara among serving plates or bowls. Garnish each serving with a sprinkle of freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese, a pinch of freshly ground black pepper, and a sprinkle of freshly chopped parsley for added freshness and color.
